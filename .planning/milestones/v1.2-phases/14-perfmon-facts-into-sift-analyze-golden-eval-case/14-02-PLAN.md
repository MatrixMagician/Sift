<assumption_delta_decision>
Transition detected: `PerfmonAnalysis` today deliberately forbids a case-level
hazard channel ("every hazard is attributable to exactly one span" — perfmon.py:178-184).
D-08 introduces a disclosure that is genuinely NOT attributable to one span — a
required→optional / one-scope→two-scope transition.

- Noun now primary: the **unattributed-samples disclosure** (perfmon samples with
  no placeable timestamp, case-wide).
- Decision: **add-alongside** (Option B). Emit a synthetic disclosure `TrendGroup`
  (`scope="file"`, `key` = a fixed sentinel, dedicated `UNATTRIBUTED_LABEL`,
  `sample_count=0`, `counters=()`, carrying only the `_hazard_unplaceable_samples`
  hazard) appended to `groups`, rather than PROMOTING `PerfmonAnalysis` to carry a
  case-level hazard field (Option A).
- Rationale: lowest blast radius. The zero-sample hazard-only `TrendGroup` shape
  already ships (`_file_scope_groups` NO_PLACEABLE case), so `sift perfmon`
  renderers need NO change and "nothing disappears silently" holds automatically;
  the "hazards live on TrendGroup / one-hazard-one-span" invariant is preserved
  (no model-field addition, no docstring-invariant rewrite). `severity="info"`
  sorts last and is dropped first under 14-03's D-03 cap — acceptable for a
  disclosure.
- Accepted debt: `scope="file"` is semantically loose for a case-wide (not
  per-file) disclosure. What would force a later promote: a downstream consumer
  that must structurally distinguish this disclosure from a real per-file trend
  (then add a dedicated `scope="unattributed"` Literal and update the exhaustive
  scope switches in `render/perfmon_report.py`). Not needed this phase.
- Contract test encoding the generalised intent: Task 2's determinism + citation
  assertions (disclosure cited, capped, event_id-sorted; two runs byte-identical).
</assumption_delta_decision>
